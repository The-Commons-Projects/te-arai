Introducing

The Commons | Te Arai

A compound that facilitates the ultimate group holiday. Pavilion house on 1-2 hectares near Te Arai. Sleeps 8-12.

Golf Saturday morning, horses on the beach, kids sorted, everyone around the fire Saturday night.

Best of all, it pays for itself.

The pavilion compound

Together, but not on top of each other.

Two bedroom wings

Each wing opens onto the central courtyard with its own entrance. One family per wing, or spread across both. Private when you want it, together when you don't.

Covered walkways

Wrap three sides of the courtyard, connecting the living pavilion to the bedroom wings and the outdoor kitchen. You're never stuck inside.

Central courtyard

The heart of the compound. 10m × 10m. Kids play during the day, adults sit around the fire at night. Doors face inward, windows outward with a view.

Three families. Three ways to use it.

All three families

Both wings full, living pavilion buzzing, courtyard chaos.

Two families

One wing each, the other stays locked. Still works perfectly.

Just yours

One wing, the whole compound to yourselves. A private retreat.

What's here

Everything that makes the compound work.

Golf simulator

Full launch monitor setup in an insulated shed. Rainy days sorted. Practice your swing while the kids are in the hot tub.

Cedar hot tub

Seats 6-8. The thing everyone ends up in by 8pm. Views across the courtyard to the fire pit.

Outdoor kitchen

Gozney pizza oven, full BBQ setup, covered prep area off the main deck. Saturday night dinner is an event.

Simple yards

Simple nag yards on site so the girls can bring the horses up. Ride the beach in the morning, rinse them off, done.

Gear room & mudroom

Riding boots, golf bag, bikes, scooters, wetsuit — all stored, organised, a lock up for our stuff. You never have to pack up.

Fire pit & covered deck

93sqm of covered outdoor living. The deck is where you spend 80% of your time. Fire pit anchors the courtyard.

Who stays here

There's no premium group accommodation in the Te Arai corridor. Te Arai Links is studio rooms for couples. Bookabach is average baches that sleep six. Nothing sleeps 8-12 at this quality level. The Commons fills the gap.

Friend groups

Long weekends, birthdays, reunions. 8-12 adults, one booking. The kind of group that currently can't find anywhere good enough that fits everyone.

Golf groups

International and domestic. Te Arai Links on the doorstep, four courses within 20 minutes, golf sim for warm-up, hot tub after. This is the high-value segment.

Families

2-3 families, school holidays, Christmas and long weekends. Kids in the courtyard, adults on the deck. The whole reason this place exists.

Corporate retreats

Off-site team days, strategy sessions, midweek overnights. 80 minutes from Auckland. Private, impressive, and doesn't feel like a conference centre.
